A coffee drinking, beanie wearing web developer.



Hi, you can call me Red!

I've always loved technology, but my journey to web development is not a direct one.

My first experience with technology came with my first console - a PS1. I was amazed that such a tiny device could produce content almost from nothing! However, computers always held my interest more than consoles. Games were better - that was reason enough for me. The concept of building my own system from scratch quickly became my goal. How hard could it be? As it turns out - hard. My first PC rattled constantly - and I still don't know why.

Now that I had a computer of my own I had to learn to manage it. "Not Responding? Why?" Fortunately these new skills were useful. I quickly became the perpetually frustrated I.T. guy. Helping others with their computer issues became a regular experience. Whilst frustrating at times, there was no greater satisfaction than overcoming the problem.

Cool, so what about now? Today I am a 24 year old web developer based in Auckland. This year I graduated from the University of Auckland with a Bachelor of Arts, double majoring in Psychology and Sociology. The course was an incredible, late-night, coffee-fueled endeavour. Whilst I enjoyed every moment, it failed capture my heart as a profession. Providence soon had an answer to my issues. In my final semester of study, I happened to forget AdBlock on a fresh system and noticed an ad. "Get your free 30 day code bootcamp!". Code! How hard could that be? I've worked with computers all my life!
No stranger to frustration and coffee-for-sleep substitutes, I juggled both university and a bootcamp. Now, many early mornings, warm beanies and empty mugs later I am building a career out of my passion.

I am a web developer. Feel free to contact me!
